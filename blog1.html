How to Store Ogbono & Egusi Without Losing Taste

Ogbono and Egusi are essential staples in many Nigerian homes. But if not stored properly, they can quickly attract insects or lose their rich flavor.

To keep ogbono fresh, always store it in a dry, airtight container away from heat and moisture. You can blend it into powder and refrigerate it for even longer shelf life. Just ensure it’s well-sealed to avoid clumping.

Egusi (melon seeds) should be kept in tightly closed containers, preferably glass or plastic jars with lids. Store in a cool cupboard or refrigerate if ground. Avoid placing them near onions or wet ingredients.

For both items, do not use nylon bags — they trap moisture. Instead, use clean dry containers, and label them with dates to monitor freshness.
