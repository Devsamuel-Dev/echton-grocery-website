How to Wash and Prep Nigerian Beans Without Sand or Stones

Washing beans can be stressful when you keep biting stones in your ewa or akara. But with these local tips, you can clean your beans quickly and safely.

First, pour the beans into a wide tray or bowl. Gently spread it out and pick out visible dirt, black beans, and stones. Then, rinse thoroughly with clean water, swirling gently to allow sand and lighter chaff to float or settle.

If you’re peeling beans for moi moi or akara, soak in warm water for 10–15 minutes and rub the beans between your palms to remove the skin. Rinse several times until the water runs clear.

For bulk prep, you can wash, dry, and store clean beans in airtight containers. It saves time when you're cooking during the week.
