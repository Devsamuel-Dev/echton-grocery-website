How to Store Crayfish, Pepper & Stockfish Without Smell

Nigerian staples like crayfish, dry pepper, and stockfish are full of flavour — but they can smell up your kitchen if not stored well. Proper storage also keeps them fresh and insect-free.

For crayfish, roast lightly in a dry pan to kill moisture, then store in airtight containers or clean bottles. Avoid leaving it in nylon, especially in humid weather.

Dry pepper should be ground, sieved, and kept in sealed jars. You can even drop a few dry cloves inside to prevent bugs and preserve the aroma.

Stockfish lasts longer when kept in a dry place. If possible, sun-dry again at home before storing in a large ziplock bag or container with holes for airflow.

With these simple steps, your local ingredients stay fresh — and your kitchen stays odor-free.
